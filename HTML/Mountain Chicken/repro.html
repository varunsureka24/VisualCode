Reproductive Behaviour of the Mountain Chicken

The mountain chicken’s form of reproduction is highly unusual. Instead of breeding in water, the frog breeds in burrows around 2ft deep, “hence the name giant ditch frog.”

The breeding season starts towards the start of the wet season and continues through September. The male will occupy a burrow and emit loud yells to attract a female mate. After breeding, the male defends the burrow while the female lays the eggs. After that, the mountain chicken does something even more unusual. The female lays up to 25,000 more unfertilized eggs on which the tadpoles feed on. Eventually, 30 - 40 froglets emerge from the burrow with a lifespan of about 12 years.
