Extinction Threats

Years of habitat loss from deforestation and volcanic eruptions have had the species' population dwindling, and it's only growing worse. In 2002 the deadly chytrid fungus (Batrachochytrium dendrobatidis) arrived in Dominica and devastated the mountain chicken population there.

Even the leading Mountain Chicken conservation group belived that they were extinct:

“At one point they thought mountain chickens were extinct on Dominica,” said Jeff Dawson, who is an amphibian program manager at the Durrell Wildlife Conservation Trust.

The fungus then migrated to Monsterrat and by 2009, wiped out more than 80% of the population there. In 2008 there was an estimation of about 8,000 wild mountain chickens, ut by 2019, that number had fallen below 200. Zoos and wildlife conservation groups have attempted to breed mountain chickens in captivity as a last-ditch attempt at preserving the population.
